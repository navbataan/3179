Green Space vs. Population Density in Melbourne & Surroundings (2024)

Exploring how green space varies across Melbourne's suburbs, from the areas in the inner city with dense populations to the outer regions, and how these patterns show the balance between population growth and preservation of green spaces.

1 · Green Space in Melbourne

Where is Melbourne's Green Space?

This map sets the scene. Darker blue tones show higher population density, while the green circles scale with the proportion of green space in each Local Government Area (LGA). We can see that the centre of Melbourne is tightly packed, with smaller circles indicating less green space, while the outer LGAs, like Yarra Ranges or Mornington Peninsula, have larger green markers and lower population density.

You can hover over the circles to see exact values for each area, and see how green space and density are balanced across the metropolitan region.

2 · Correlation

Do Denser Areas Have Less Green Space?

Each dot here represents one Victorian LGA. On the x-axis is population density, and on the y-axis is the green space percentage. There is a somewhat clear pattern with density rising and green space generally falling.

The scatterplot allows you to hover to explore different LGAs, you'll find that outer suburbs and regional councils cluster towards the top left (low density, high green space), while inner-city LGAs sit near the bottom right (high density, limited greenery).

The filter tab allows you to see the difference between the single regional area included in the nearby suburbs to Melbourne and the rest of the metropolitan LGAs. This shows that regional areas do not always have the highest green space, it is very depedent on how they use their open space.

3 · Leading and Lagging LGAs

Which LGAs Stand Out?

These charts reveal the top and bottom LGAs, by both green space and population density. Here, we can see that coastal and outer areas usually have higher green space percentages while the inner city LGAs such as Melbourne and Port Phillip are among the most densely populated.

Yarra Ranges can be seen on both bar charts to have the highest green space percentage and the lowest population density, emphasising that correlation. This highlights the trade-offs between accessibility, housing pressure, and open space availability in inner city areas which are more convenient for most people.

4 · Distribution

How Is Green Space Distributed?

The box plot essentially summarises how green space varies within low, medium, and high-density LGAs. The wide range in the low-density areas reflects that Melbourne has a mix of semi-rural and suburban landscapes surrounding the city suburbs.

5 · Overall Picture

The Variety in Melbourne's Green Spaces

On the left, we can see all of Melbourne's LGAs and the distribution of green space present within them. with most LGAs have moderate green space coverage, but a few outliers stand out as particularly green or built-up. We can see that most of Melbourne's inner suburbs have around 10-30% green space in them, a relatively low value considering the emphasis on sustainability by Victoria.

The data reminds us that urban sustainability is not just about numbers, it's about livability, equity, and access to nature.
